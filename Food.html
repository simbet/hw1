I know what's on your mind: German haute cuisine is centered around

  • Sausages
  • Sauerkraut
  • Beer
...all three preferably served in a Stein. Yes, we do love those but every once in a while we spice up our F&B intake with alternative breakfast, lunch or dinner options. 😉

Tell me fellow foodies, have you heard of "Sonntagsfrühstück", "Rostbraten mit Spätzle & Soß" or "Schweinshaxe mit Breznknödel & Dunkelbiersoß"?
Too meaty? Fear not my vegetarian friends - I got you covered with "Schupfnudeln mit Kirschtomaten, Spinat und Sahnesoße" and for dessert "Dampfnudel mit Vanillesoß".

Whichever of those dishes I can get my hands on, the verdict is always the same: "Da könnt I mi neilege", i.e. "this is so good, I could fill my bathtub with it and just lay in it".

Sonntagsfrühstück

A typical Sunday breakfast would offer a mix of cured meats and cheese from your local butcher alongside breadrolls, croissants and other pastries.


Rostbraten mit Spätzle & Soß

A typical dish from southern Germany, this one is one of my all time favourites! It's a sirloin steak with onions, home made Swabian style pasta and a dark rich gravy.


Schweinshaxe mit Breznknödel & Dunkelbiersoß

A typical dish from Bavaria, this is not for the faint hearted! It's a pork knuckle, the meat is usually marinated for days, in the case of big cuts up to a week. The Schweinshaxe is then roasted at low temperatures, typically—depending on size—for two to three hours. Served with bavarian style dumplings and a rich beer gravy.


Schupfnudeln mit Kirschtomaten, Spinat und Sahnesoße

A typical dish from southern Germany, this veggie treat combines home made pasta (similar to Gnocci) with cherry tomatoes, spinach, onions and a creamy gravy.


Damdfnudeln mit Vanillesoße

A typical dessert from southern Germany, these yeast dumplings are served warm with vanilla sauce.
